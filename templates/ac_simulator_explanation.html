The simulator does not use precise math in contrast to the calculator. For these complex scenarios, we simulate a player rolling down. We do this 1000 times and count how many times we successfully hit the desired units. This is also known as Monte-Carlo-Simulation.

Our simulations are very simple. In contrast to real players, our simulations do not improve the odds of hitting a unit, by buying out units of the same tier while rolling down. Also, imagine you want to roll down on 4 and hit 6 copies of a tier 1 unit. Our simulations do not give you any information on how many units you hit in the failed runs. One more weakness is, that our simulations can not look for two possible sets of units to hit. What does that mean? Often players will roll down and not look for precisely one set of units, but two. As an example a player might be looking for Neeko and Shi Oh Yu or he could be looking for a Sy'Fen. We can approximate the probability of hitting either of the two sets by calculating the chances separately and adding them up at the end. This does disregard buying cost sligthly, but it should still offer a good approximation.
